Forests are essential for biodiversity, carbon storage, and water regulation.

Deforestation causes soil erosion by exposing bare ground.
Deforestation leads to increased carbon dioxide emissions as stored carbon is released.
Deforestation results in the loss of biodiversity by destroying natural habitats.
Deforestation disrupts the natural water cycle, reducing rainfall and water quality.
Deforestation displaces local communities, undermining traditional livelihoods.
Deforestation produces significant habitat loss for native species, contributing to species decline.
Deforestation gives rise to environmental degradation and accelerates climate change.
Deforestation can also trigger economic hardships by affecting eco-tourism and forest-based industries.
Deforestation may lead to long-term social conflicts over land and resource rights.
